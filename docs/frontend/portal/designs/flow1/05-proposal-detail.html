Blockchain-based Patient Identity Verification

A decentralized approach to patient records ensuring data integrity and preventing identity fraud across regional clinics, integrating seamlessly with the proposed infrastructure upgrade.

Dr. Sarah Chen

Toronto, CA

Linked RFP

Digital Health Records Infrastructure

Overview

The current healthcare infrastructure faces significant challenges regarding patient identity verification, leading to fragmented records, duplicated treatments, and potential medical errors. This proposal outlines a robust, scalable solution utilizing blockchain technology to create a secure, immutable, and universally accessible patient identity framework.

By leveraging a decentralized ledger, we aim to empower patients with ownership of their medical identities while providing healthcare providers with instant, verified access to crucial health data, regardless of the physical clinic location.

Technical Approach

  • Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs): Implementing W3C standard DIDs to ensure each patient has a unique, cryptographically secure identifier independent of any central registry.
  • Zero-Knowledge Proofs: Utilizing ZK-SNARKs to allow patients to prove their identity or specific attributes (e.g., age, insurance status) without revealing underlying sensitive data.
  • Interoperability Layer: Developing APIs that seamlessly interface with existing Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems used by regional clinics.

Expected Outcomes

Data Integrity

99.9% reduction in duplicate patient records across participating networks.

Cost Efficiency

Estimated 30% reduction in administrative costs related to identity verification.

Patient Security

Elimination of single points of failure for massive healthcare data breaches.

Rapid Onboarding

Streamlined patient registration process reducing wait times by up to 50%.
