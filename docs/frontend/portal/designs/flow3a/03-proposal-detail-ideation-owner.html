Urban Green Space Revitalization in District 4

Diaspora Health Network • Created Oct 24, 2023

Response to: Modernizing Diaspora Health Services

Short Description

A comprehensive initiative to transform unused public lots in District 4 into sustainable community gardens and recreational spaces, promoting physical health and community engagement among diaspora families and local residents.

Detailed Proposal

1. Executive Summary

This proposal outlines a strategic approach to revitalize three major abandoned lots in District 4. By transforming these areas into functional green spaces, we aim to improve air quality, provide safe recreational zones, and foster a stronger sense of community.

2. Objectives

  • Convert 15,000 sq ft of unused land into usable park space.
  • Install 3 community gardens managed by local diaspora volunteers.
  • Reduce local urban heat island effect by 2% within the first year.

3. Implementation Plan

The project will be executed in three phases over 18 months, starting with site clearing and soil testing, followed by infrastructure development (pathways, lighting), and concluding with planting and community handover.
